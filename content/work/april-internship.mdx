## Context

Project engineer internship at APRIL Group (Jun–Sep 2025) in pulp-and-paper manufacturing — process optimization and environmental compliance in the pulping process.

## Problem

Machine utilization and production efficiency were constrained by process bottlenecks and weak daily target-and-review discipline.

## Role

Intern-level ownership of analysis and recommendations: observe, measure, model, and present improvements to plant teams. Not full-time plant ownership.

## Approach

- On-site process observation, time studies, and root-cause review of downtime
- **Discrete-event simulation (DES)** used to raise machine utilization by **~32%**, with an **estimated** efficiency opportunity **exceeding $100K/month** (internal estimate — not an audited realized savings figure)
- Ran a **Kaizen** daily target-and-review cadence associated with **~10%** production efficiency gain over **3 months**

## Constraint

Confidential operating details stay off the public portfolio; metrics stated as estimates where realization was not independently audited.

## Result

Process and operations improvement recommendations presented to plant teams, grounded in simulation and a sustained review cadence. Metrics aligned with career timeline (user-confirmed for public use).
