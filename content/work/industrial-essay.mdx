## Context

Academic essay exploring systems thinking as a practical lens for industrial engineering problems.

## Problem

Industrial engineering often defaults to linear, single-step optimization — optimizing one variable without accounting for system-wide interactions and feedback loops.

## Role

Sole author of a structured writing sample: literature → case analysis → proposed framework.

## Approach

- Literature review of systems thinking theory and industrial engineering applications
- Manufacturing case analysis comparing linear vs. systems approaches
- Synthesis into an applied systems-thinking framework for process improvement

## Constraint

Academic writing sample — not operational implementation or validated in a live environment.

## Result

Argument for systems thinking over linear single-step optimization. Demonstrates structured analytical writing and ability to synthesize跨学科 concepts into a coherent framework.
